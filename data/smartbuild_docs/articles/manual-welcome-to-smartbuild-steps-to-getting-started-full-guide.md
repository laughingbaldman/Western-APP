Getting Started with SmartBuild
Rolling out SmartBuild – Who, What and When
Thank you for making the decision to choose SmartBuild. This document will serve as a guide that
outlines what needs to be done and how to do it. It is important that the right people in your
organization are selected to accomplish the various tasks needed.

One person, such as a single person contractor could potentially fill the requirements needed to
implement SmartBuild. But, it’s a big plus to have an internal team along with the help of SmartBuild’s
technical team. With a strong push and the right team in place, SmartBuild should produce comparable
quote outputs in a few weeks.

SmartBuild integration will require the following ingredients and effort from your organization to roll
out efficiently:

● Project Management: (~5-10 hours)
An organized, technically skilled and knowledgeable person to push the project is highly
desirable. The job of the Project Manager is to identify tasks, assign to the right individual,
provide resources, and track progress.
● Pricing and Accounting: (~15-25 hours):
SmartBuild requires an Excel file of the materials and pricing used to quote and order barns.
Usually an IT individual who is familiar with the accounting system is tasked with downloading
an inventory database which is modified and then loaded up to SmartBuild. Pricing is similarly
uploaded. When properly implemented, this process will result in “single button click” where a
dealer creates a job, clicks to make it an order, and it seamlessly loads into your accounting
software.
● Field Practices and Model: (~15-20):
It is important to involve a field expert with technical skills or a combination of individuals to
integrate field practices into the 3D model and electronic outputs. This individual will be tasked
with creating framing standards and “packages” which automate the takeoff of materials not
modeled in SmartBuild.
If you're a one-man company, these 3 above references may be the one and the same person . . YOU.
Either way SmartBuild will require some level of sustained energy to maintain, manage and use the
system to its fullest. With an initial push SmartBuild will quickly become a part of your organization and
prove to be a strong technology partner.

To reiterate, it is feasible to implement SmartBuild within a few weeks if the right people are assigned to
the required tasks and given time to accomplish those tasks. The following document shows in great
detail what needs to be done.

A Note about Terminology
Within the industry, and sometimes within a single company, multiple names are sometimes used to
describe a single type of object. For example, the terms “header” and “truss carrier” are often used
interchangeably, as well as the terms “post” and “column.” When it comes to naming the materials to
be imported into your custom version of the SmartBuild materials database, we ask that users follow the
naming conventions outlined within this document. This will ensure that all the 2D and 3D renderings,
material takeoffs and quotes generated by SmartBuild are accurate. Rest assured that, once your
materials have been entered into the database, SmartBuild provides all of the tools needed to name
your materials in any way you choose, both within the system displays and in your outputs!

Default “Generic” Database and File Templates (CSV or XLSX files)
Your account comes pre-loaded with a generic database in SmartBuild prior to you customizing/using
the data. We have populated the colors and material databases with some default data, though you may
recognize some popular brands.

There are a couple of reasons we do this. SmartBuild requires data to actually use the software. Having
this default data, will allow your company to use the software prior to completing the creation of your
actual database in the software, though this is discouraged as some companies start using it before it’s
ready and never go back and thoroughly complete the setup, resulting in an unfinished product.

Secondly, SmartBuild can only recognize data in a particular format. To help get the format correct, we
have detailed descriptions in this document. However, sometimes it’s more helpful to have an actual
template with which you can start the process of creating your actual database. SmartBuild gives you
the option of downloading a file you can use as a template. This default data will also be downloaded,
giving you an even better sample of what you need to create to populate your database.

0.3 – Skus in All Categories
There are three Skus referenced in SmartBuild: Sku, Vendor Sku and Supplier Sku. The easiest and
fastest way to complete a setup for lumber, sheathing, and trim may very well be taking the generic
SmartBuild Sku, and if your company’s existing Sku format is similar, you can simply change the
SmartBuild Sku to yours, tweak the description and then verify the available lengths. This technique is
not always possible and may not always be able to be utilized. We will explain this a little more in depth
in each section

The only required Sku is Sku. SmartBuild uses this Sku to uniquely identify every piece of material
(expanded for colors and part lengths). This means that EVERY piece of material must have a unique Sku
within SmartBuild.

The Vendor Sku is not required, but it can provide a helpful link between the SmartBuild system and
other software systems you may use (for accounting, inventory, ordering, etc.). Example: if your
products use a Sku such as 987654 for a 2x4 -16’ then you definitely must use a Vendor Sku. If you use a
Sku system similar to SmartBuild though, where this item Sku is 2X416, then Vendor Sku could very well
be left blank.

Also note: The Vendor Sku can be the same for multiple entries in the database only IF the pricing is the
same for these multiple items.

0.4 – Understanding the SmartBuild Sku (mainly for creating an upload file)
As previously mentioned SmartBuild requires a UNIQUE identifier for EVERY piece of material. This
means that for one piece of material that comes in two different lengths and 5 different colors, we need
10 different Skus to identify that piece of material (because in reality it is 10 different pieces of
material). The SmartBuild Sku was created to help with that situation. When adding material via the
ADD button, you can let SmartBuild create this Sku. However, if you want to generate the list in a file to
be uploaded, then you must create the SmartBuild Sku to be included in the file.

With the simplest format, SmartBuild Sku can be set, and the Vendor Sku left blank. This is for items that
are sold as individual pieces (each) … no variations because of additional lengths or colors.
If there is a Vendor Sku in the exported source file/list it can be used as the basis for the SmartBuild Sku.
It will overwrite it and be displayed on your order lists. Additional information may be needed to create
the new SmartBuild Sku for the exceptions mentioned (sheathing, trim and fasteners).

To make this document a little easier to understand, we’ll assume that a pre-existing Vendor Sku will be
used as the base for creating the SmartBuild Sku. However, as I’ve already mentioned this isn’t required.
If you don’t have (or want to use) Vendor Skus, you can generate the SmartBuild Sku from scratch (see
paragraph 0.5 Sku Best Practices to help). You can come up with your own structure as long as you
follow the guidelines below.
1. Don’t use < > and &
2. Only use the strings {LF} or {CC} in the Sku when appropriate (to designate length or color)
For material that comes in multiple colors and/or multiple part lengths, SmartBuild will create the new
Sku using {CC} and {LF} tokens to replace color codes or part lengths. Below are various possibilities
when creating a sheathing Sku:
● M1{CC}{LF} – Material that comes in both multiple colors and predefined part lengths. e.g. the
Sku for a red 10’ piece of material could be ProfileRD10 where color code RD replaced the {CC},
and predefined part length 10 replaced the {LF}

● ItemTwo{CC} – Material that comes in multiple colors, and either one predefined part length, or
sold by the foot (Random Length). e.g. The Sku for Red material sold by the foot could be
ItemWord where RD replaced the {CC}
● ItemThree{LF} – Material sold in multiple predefined part lengths, but not multiple colors. e.g.
The Sku for material sold in 10’ and 12” predefined lengths could be ItemThree10 and
ItemThree12, where 10 and 12 replaced the {LF}

Framing material can be sold by the foot or by the piece. The SmartBuild Sku definition is influenced by

Material sold by fixed predefined part lengths is referred to as Part Length (P/L). Material sold in part
lengths, will be priced per piece. SmartBuild will calculate how many pieces are needed, and using the
price per item (each), will calculate the total price of the job.

Material sold by the foot is referred to as Random Length (R/L). Since Random Length material is sold by
the foot, SmartBuild will calculate the total LF needed of that material, and use the per foot price to
calculate the total price for that material.

Trim. The following 2 paragraphs will explain the difference between Part Length to Random Length.

If the Vendor Sku does NOT have the linear feet embedded in it, then copy the Vendor Sku to the
SmartBuild Sku, and append {LF} to it.

Any reference to linear feet in the Material column in the new file should be removed so this field will be
exactly the same for every entry with the same SmartBuild Sku.

5.2 – Trim – Part Length vs Random Length
Trim can be sold by the foot, by the piece, and in multiple colors. The SmartBuild Sku definition is
influenced by each of these factors.
Material sold by fixed predefined part lengths is referred to as Part Length (P/L). Material sold in part
lengths will be priced per piece. SmartBuild will calculate how many pieces are needed, and using the
price per item (each), will calculate the total price of the job.

Material sold by the foot is referred to as Random Length (R/L). Since Random Length material is sold by
the foot, SmartBuild will calculate the total LF needed of that material and use the per foot price to
calculate the total price for that material.

SmartBuild defaults Trim material to 10’ Part Lengths. However, you can also enter Random Length
Trim. The next paragraph will explain how to change the default definition of Part Length to Random
Length.

Step 13: Sheathing Assemblies
Sheathing Assemblies working alongside Trim Assemblies and Product Systems, are probably the most
crucial parts of the SmartBuild set-up in order to have the most complete and advanced SmartBuild
implementation to date. SmartBuild can work with older and dated processes such as using Packages
but not to the level of accuracy we have seen with Assemblies.
So just what is an assembly and how does it work??
An “assembly” is a list of items you can group together so they can be applied to an area of the project
such as the Wall Sheathing. Currently as of version 1.94 , SmartBuild only shows the primary layer of an
assembly in the 3D model, so in this example you only see the Ag Panel, even if you look inside the
model, the House Wrap being added via an assembly is not visible.

The main focus is having an accurate material list, that includes not only the steel cut to the correct
lengths, but also underlayment products, fasteners, nails, and screws, all included as part of the
complete materials list. After these have been set up once, the salesperson can simply select an
assembly to include multiple items with any given project.

The biggest advantage you have with assemblies, in any given project you can select a single wall or roof
plane and apply a different assembly to JUST that area, to be able to handle more complex customer
requests.

**If you are transitioning from using primary sheathing and trim parts by themselves to Assemblies
instead, we have some automatic processes that are in place to make a smooth transition, HOWEVER
this can cause issues. If you have been using SmartBuild for a while and decide to start setting up
Assemblies, it will “steal” the usage from the primary material, so all existing jobs will use the first new
assembly that you created. We highly recommend that the first assembly you create with each trim is a
very basic assembly only including the fasteners in it. Following that you can copy and create more
versions of that assembly, adding additional upgrades to it. When you reopen old jobs, the only extra
material you would be getting is “old package fasteners” and if that package has been deleted,
everything just works the first time the job is refreshed.

Some common assemblies to set up would be:
● Ag panel with Standard Fasteners
● Ag panel with Vapor Barrier and Standard Fasteners
● Standing Seam with OSB, Underlayment, Clips, and Screws

18.3 Packages - Notes
In order to estimate a material from your master material database, you need to first have the material
added to your SmartBuild material database. If all your materials have not been loaded yet, please
return to that category and follow the steps needed to load materials, Steps 2-17.

The way a material is entered into the system will affect how a Package is set up. If you set the price of a
material by the roll (or box or bag…) then the Add -on will need to figure how many rolls are needed for
a given job. To figure this, you need to find out what percentage of a roll is used based on your selected
‘Calc Base’, such as square foot of wall. If you don’t want the program to call out a roll or box etc. but
instead just want the lineal footage or square footage of material per job, then you would price the
material based on square foot or lineal foot (not per roll, box….). So, if a 100 foot roll cost $100 dollars,
then the material would be priced as $1.00 to get a cost per linear foot. So, the way a material is priced
needs to coordinate with the way you want to report and estimate the material.

Example 1: Estimate per roll based on Lineal foot
Let’s say you use a roll on waterproof flashing for all windows. You need to have a material in your
database to use. In your database, you add a 50’ roll of flashing priced per roll in your ‘Window
Hardware’ category. In this case, you want to figure out how many rolls to send out to any given job. So
you create a ‘Base Macro’ to account for this flashing material. In this case, the best Calc Base available
is ‘Lineal Foot of Window Trim’. So, how do we figure out how many 50’ rolls we need per job?

If you want to use 1 lineal foot of flashing for each 1’ of window trim then you can use this formula:
{LFOpeningPerimeter}divided by 50’ of flashing


This means for every lineal foot of window trim for a given job, you will get 1 foot of window flashing,
but it will only report the number of rolls required for the material take off. If a job has less than 50
lineal feet of window trim then one roll of ‘Roll Tape’’ will be included and priced on the material take-
off. As the lineal feet of window trim increases so does the number of rolls, but only when it reaches a

===== PAGE 122 =====

122

point that requires a new roll. So, 49’ will require 1 roll (the program will round up when exactly 50’). 50’
will require 2 rolls. 100’ will require 3 rolls etc.
You can build waste factors into the calculations. For example, one way to handle this is to add 10% to
the lineal footage of trim and then figure out the material based on actual lineal footage plus a 10%
waste factor. The calculation would look like: ({LFOpeningPerimeter}*1.1)/50. This calculation finds 10%
of the lineal footage of trim and then adds the actual lineal footage of window trim and then figures out
how many rolls are required based on the waste adjusted lineal footage.

This is one example, but the same principles can apply to any type of material where you need to find
the quantity of rolls, or bags or boxes to send out per lineal foot of something.

18.5 - Using Excel Functions in SmartBuild Package Calculations
You can use many of the functions available in Excel within the calculation used to call out materials in
SmartBuild (see end of this document for a full list of available Excel functions). If you have an Excel
expert then they would be a good resource to help create these types of packages for your business.

IF Function
The most valuable function is the IF function. You can use IF functions within package calculations to
evaluate certain conditions, and then perform different calculations depending on whether the test was
TRUE or FALSE. These are very similar to the IF functions used in Excel. The only difference, unlike Excel,
SmartBuild cannot reference other cells in a spreadsheet. Instead, SmartBuild can reference ‘Calc Bases’
which represent information from the job like total square footage, total weight and many more.

Note: the purpose of an individual calculation is to add a calculated quantity (and therefore a total
cost/price) of a single material to a job. So, it is important to understand that the way the material is
added in the Pricing database will affect how the calculations is set up.

Example 1
You can use a single IF function, but the most value comes from ‘nested’ IF functions. A "nested IF"
refers to a formula where more than one IF function is nested inside another to check more than one
condition. The first function found to be true will be used for the calculation.

General Syntax:
IF(logical_test, value_if_true,IF(logical_test, value_if_true ))
Example IF statement:
if({TotalWeight} <=3000,1,if({TotalWeight} <=6000,2,if({TotalWeight} <=9000,3)))

===== PAGE 127 =====

127

In this example, say I have a delivery fee in my material database of $100. The way this is set up, if the
total weight of the job is equal to or less than (<=) 3000 pounds, then I will get a quantity of 1 delivery
fee, so $100. If the statement is not true (i.e. the total weight is more than 3000) then it will move on to
the next If statement. If the total weight is less than or equal to 6000 pounds then I will get a quantity of
2, so $200 will be placed on the job’s material list. (Note: since the function already determined that <=
3000 was false, so even though this 2nd function includes anything below 6000, we know it is not below
3000 already). If the total weight is less than or equal to 9000 then I will get a quantity of 3 delivery fees
(or $300). We could continue to nest more total weights to make sure we cover all the possibilities.

Each IF statement needs to be carefully "nested" inside another so the calculation will execute correctly.
It is required to include the correct number of parentheses in the function. For each nested IF
statement, an opening and a closing parenthesis is required. Notice in the above ‘Example 2’, each IF
statement has an open parenthesis before the test and then all three are closed off at the end with 3
closing parenthesis. Also, be careful to check the commas. Commas are used to separate the test from
the value:

e.g. {TotalWeight} <=3000,1.
And a comma is used to separate each nested If statement:
e.g. {TotalWeight} <=3000,1,if({TotalWeight} <=6000,2….
If any statement has incorrect parentheses and/or commas then all packages in the system will not
function and they will all report a quantity of 1. The program will give you an error message in Test
Mode and point you to the error.

if({SFMain} > 4002,1.75,
))))))))

18.6 - Recommendations (Do’s and Don’ts):
Below is a list of recommendations for Packages.
● DON'T create a Group if there's only one package. This simply confuses the user by implying that
they have a choice.
● DON'T repeat the Group name in each Package. This just wastes space in the interface.
● DON'T require the user to select or type in a qty that is available as a calc base. If the user has a
chance to answer a question wrong, they will.
● DO use the calc bases and the IF function liberally. This is particularly helpful when there are
scenarios like varying labor rates for different ranges of square footage.
● DON'T use Miscellaneous materials with prices in your packages. By adding a Material to your
database, all pricing changes can be handled in one place. You can use Miscellaneous entries
when you just want to verify a quantity (see the tip below about "dummy" entries).
● DON'T make the group names or package names too long. Test the package to make sure none
of the text is getting truncated.
● DO include Info Text, especially if you need to shorten the name. This is also useful for
"checklist" packages, to explain to the user what they should be looking for.
● DON'T add ‘?’ to the end of a question. It won't appear in the dialog.
● DO test each package using the Test button when creating the calculation.
● DO arrange your calculations so that you can easily tell what they're supposed to be doing.
Instead of {SFRoof}*0.002 for a product that is 4' wide and 125' long, use {SFRoof}/(4*125). You
WILL look at this in 6 months and scratch your head.
● DO let us know if there's a calc base you'd like to have. We can usually get these added in a
week or so.

● The truss carrier material, also known as the “header” or the “top girt” is the part where the

● There are three options for the wall gable wall style:
✔ Full height option has no trusses at the end of the building and the posts and girts run all
the way up to the top of the roof.
✔ Gable end is where there is a gable truss and the posts run all the way up to the roof
line.
✔ The gable short post option has the post stopping at the bottom of truss.
● Purlins style also have three types
✔ The flat option lays flat on top of the truss.
✔ The on edge will be in the strong axis on top of the trusses.
✔ The flush options will cut around the purlins in between each truss and will mount flush
with the top of the truss using hangers.
● Girts have two styles; standard and bookshelf that get cut in between the posts and laid flush
with the inside and outside of the post
● The skirt-board material can be stacked and aligned with the building grade so that half the
skirt-board is above grade and the other half is below grade.
✔ You can also set the line of which the base trim will be set up in the bottom of the wall
steel will sit.
● Roof ledger is the part where the rafters for the attached building get tied into the wall of the
main building.

To manually add a Post Assembly to your SmartBuild materials database, open the Setup Wizard to the
Post Assemblies tab, as pictured in Figure 1 on the previous page. Once there, begin adding a new Post
Assembly by clicking on the Plus button (+) on the toolbar, as pictured in Figure 2 below.
This will open the Add New Post Assemblies dialog box. (Figure 3 below.) Take a moment to familiarize
yourself with its contents. There are three main sections: the Field Definitions at the top, the Usages
section in the middle, and the Entries section at the bottom. We will discuss each of these sections in
turn, as we use them to define a new Post Assembly.

Field Definitions
In the field definitions section at the top of the dialog (Figure 4), complete each of the fields as
described below. Note that only three fields are required, as indicated by the red asterisks (Name,
Primary Material and Foundation Type):
● Key – Leave this field blank. SmartBuild will automatically enter Key data here.
● Name – Enter a unique name for this Post Assembly. This name will be visible in the user
interface and available as a {token} for use in your bid outputs.
● Primary Material – Use the drop-down list to select the material to be used for the main post
section within the exterior walls.
● Foundation Type – Use the drop-down list to select the type of building foundation to be
associated with this new Post Assembly. The options include Post Hole, Stem Wall, Thick Edge
Slab and None.
● Foundation Width – If you have selected the Post Hole Foundation Type, enter the hole
diameter in this field. For Stem Walls, specify the wall thickness. For Foundation Types
designated Thick Edge Slab or None, leave the value set at 0’.
● Foundation Height – This field is only visible if Stem Wall is selected as the Foundation Type.
Enter the height of your stem wall from bottom to top.
● Foundation Depth – For Post Holes, specify the hole depth. For Stem Walls, specify the wall
depth below ground. For Foundation Types Thick Edge Slab and None, leave the value set at 0’.
● Footing Width – This field is only visible if Stem Wall or Thick Edge Slab are selected as the
Foundation Type. Specify the width of the grade beam or spread footing located at the bottom
of the stem wall or thick edge.
● Footing Depth – This field is only visible if Stem Wall or Thick Edge Slab are selected as the
Foundation Type. Specify the depth of the grade beam or spread footing located at the bottom
of the stem wall or thick edge.

Special Conditions
The Special Conditions section of this dialog box is used to define restrictions on when a material is to be
added as a component the Post Assembly being defined. Special Conditions are an advanced
configuration feature that lie beyond the scope of this introductory document. For guidance on using
Special Features in your Post Assembly configurations, please contact SmartBuild support.
Save/Cancel
When you are done configuring your new Post Assembly, click on the Save button in the dialog box to
add to the Entries for this Post Assemblies. Click on the Cancel button to close the dialog box and discard
the Entries. Back in the main Add New Post Assemblies dialog box, do the same, clicking Save to add
your new Post Assembly to the list, or cancel to discard it.
